Key Takeaways per Module

Introduction

  • Problem Solving, wherein you can focus 95% on the solutions and 5% on the problems, can help improve performance in an internship or job.
  • Solution-Focused Mindset it emphasizes concentrating mostly of your efforts on finding solutions.

Module 1

  • Talks about ones strength and weaknesses, self improvement, and how I want to be known for and stand out within the work community.
  • Knowing my boss/superviser and how to approach them, their working style, and how to talk to them.
  • How to be a valuable employee and the skills and qualities you will need to work on.

Module 2

  • Fostering collaboration and innovation, cach contribution, no matter how small, can lead to a more dynamic and supportive work environment.
  • How one can stand out, introvert or extrovert and what can an employee do to support the business, boss, and team.

Module 3

  • Focuses on the key factors and behaviors that helps you stand out within an organization
  • It introduces the concept of "GEM" Global, Executive, and Mindset—which plays a vital role in connecting and communicating with others confidently and personally.
  • Covers the essential skills for effective communication, helping you get your message across more clearly, and provides strategies for building trust with others.
  • How to give direct feedback, including the appropriate language to use, to ensure clarity and impact.
  • Discusses TILT ( Takes Action, Inspires Others, Lends a Hand, and Teaches.) The module emphasizes how to take ownership by practicing these principles, as well as how to coach others and offer constructive feedback to foster growth and recognition.

5C's Reflection

How did your service learning experience contribute to the development of your 5C's?

By actively promoting the library, I developed a deeper understanding of the resources and services available, which enhanced my ability to support others in using them effectively.

How did the tasks and challenges you encountered during your service learning experience enhance your knowledge and skills?

I became more skilled in researching, curating materials, and identifying what resources would benefit the school community.

In what ways did your service learning experience help you strengthen your work habits, respect for others, and organisational skills, and how did these aspects of your character contribute to your overall performance?

My experience with promoting the library helped me develop responsibility, as I took ownership of ensuring that students and teachers were aware of what more the library can offer and utilize the tools.

How did your service learning experience improve your ability to collaborate with others, including working harmoniously in a team, resolving conflicts, and contributing effectively to group efforts?

The process and planning of promoting the library required working closely with our librarians and fellow students, which taught me the importance of teamwork and communication.

How did your service learning experience influence your ability to demonstrate commitment to achieving goals?

My commitment to this task handed to me and my classmates helped ensure that the promotion efforts had lasting impact, benefiting both students and staff.

How did your service learning experience enhance your ability to think creatively and innovate?

The lesson to my service learning is making the most of existing resources, which encouraged me to think creatively about how to use them in new ways. For example, using digital tools or repurposing old materials for events not only stretched resources but also helped me see the potential to innovate the areas I hadn't previously considered.

Grade 12 - Key Takeaways

Module 4

  • I understood both the formal (“written”) and informal (“unwritten”) norms in a workplace, what’s explicitly stated vs what’s implicit.
  • I learned that storytelling is a powerful credibility tool and to use stories to make connections, to illustrate your point, and to build trust.
  • Pay attention to details: the small things (how you introduce yourself, your manners) make a big difference in how you’re perceived.

Module 5

  • This module goes through different kinds of situations you might face. With superiors, peers, or during meetings/projects.
  • Module 5 teaches how to collaborate, build trust, and communicate effectively with coworkers.
  • We learn how to behave, contribute, and influence in meetings; how to take initiative or navigate group projects.

Module 6

  • Module 6 is about long-term career visibility: consistent networking and relationship-building contribute to long-term success, not just short-term gains.
  • The module emphasizes “power skills” — the interpersonal and communication abilities that help you become visible and trusted.
  • Strategic relationship building: how to know who to connect with, when to connect, and how to build meaningful professional relationships.

5C's Reflection

How did your service learning experience contribute to the development of your 5C's?

My service-learning experience from last academic year helped me grow in all areas of the 5Cs — Competence, Character, Commitment to Achieve, Collaboration, and Creativity and Innovation. Working with the library allowed me to apply what I’ve learned to situations in real life. It taught me how to communicate effectively, manage my time, and adapt to challenges. More importantly, it helped me understand the value of teamwork, empathy, and persistence. The experience was not just about helping others but also about discovering my potential and how I can make a difference through my actions.

How did your service learning experience contribute to the development of your 5C's?

Throughout my experience last academic year, I developed both technical and interpersonal skills. I learned how to plan and execute tasks efficiently, especially how to navigate google sheets.

How did your service learning experience contribute to the development of your 5C's?

This experience strengthened my discipline, patience, and respect for others. Working with different kinds of people taught me to listen and understand perspectives that differ from mine. I learned that respect and professionalism are essential in maintaining harmony within a group and with the community.

How did your service learning experience contribute to the development of your 5C's?

My service-learning experience before taught me the true value of teamwork and communication. I learned how to divide responsibilities fairly, support my mates, and remain open to feedback.

How did your service learning experience contribute to the development of your 5C's?

Doing my service learning required dedication and follow-through, even when things became confusing. There were times when I felt overwhelmed with the amount of books I needed to encode, but remembering the impact of our work kept me motivated.

How did your service learning experience contribute to the development of your 5C's?

Finally, the experience from last year and my soon to be service learning in Munich encouraged me to think outside the box. Some challenges we faced require creative solutions — such as finding new ways to engage people or adjusting the workload to fit unexpected situations. These moments helped me realize that innovation often comes from necessity and teamwork. I became more confident in suggesting ideas and finding practical yet imaginative ways to make our project more effective and meaningful.
